I tend to hack everything: from hardware, through software and 3d graphics, to cuisines. I follow "build before buy" rule. Whatever I need I buy it only if I can't build it. The rule applies to dumping as well. I treat devices as pieces of art, so they get another chance before disposal, and they often measure up to the new role.

Hardware Projects

The Measure

The vintage electric current meters spent their entire lives on military warehouse shelves. They were about to be thrown away. I decided they look to cool to end in the trash yet. That was right. Now the gauges happily jump back and forth as though their sole reason of existence was a performance.

The Tube

The ventilation tubes were taking a lot of space around the renovation site while waiting to take over their true job. Nobody expected they can do anything else. At the moment they enjoy their side job, a shop window mascot.

Stork

There was no utility for the vintage flight recorder in the digital age any more. The super durable tape recorder was on its way to the dumpster. Someone had to take an effort to unscrew its hermetic case to disover its surprising beauty. New triangular skin and three power banks just backup the rolling performance.

3D Graphics

Genealogy Tree

I asked my mom about one of my far relatives and she did not offer a lot of information. I started to search by myself. From city to city, from person to person, from archive to archive, the family story suddenly exploded with faces, lives and even secrets. My PC was happy to handle the overwhelming number of relations and render them on one, huge picture.

Centennial Hall

The building was erected 100 years ago by our then enemy, to commemorate its battlefield triumph. Fast forward several decades and the monumental construction is now in our posession, and the former enemy is now our friend. To share its beauty I decided the Hall deserves its place not only in our physical space but also in memories of our contemporary, digital toys. They don't know the borders. We should not either!

Gas Tower

Most towers were built to emphasize the power of their owners. This one used to hold water by harnessing the power of gravity. The industrial genesis did not prevent it from being handsome. Its current purpose is unknown, so the four huge, round, look-through windows seem to promise some distant mystery.

Delicious Treats

Ice Cream Cake

The cold ice cream is just a camouflage to the true core boiling inside the volcano-like cake. The hot chocolate lava is just waiting to be uncovered by a unaware victim greedy for the strawberry. Tons of calories inevitably ends up in the body, misleading its commanding mind with the pure pleasure.

Holeless Donut

Fat Thursday is a great excuse. I would not omit the chance. A vanilla cream and its friend 20% Advocaat make sure the sin will be complete. They hide inside the sweet soft bun, covered with a chocolate glaze. Bon apetit!

Ginger Cakes

How not to be a gourmand? Preparing the ginger cookies is labor-intensive and pleasant at the same time. The baking process releases the lovely spicy scent that fills the house and reminds of the coming holidays. My mom used to bake ginger cookies every year. I obey the tradition.
